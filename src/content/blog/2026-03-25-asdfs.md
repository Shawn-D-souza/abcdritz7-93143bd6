## Why Is Sophia the Face of a New Era?
The world is no longer waiting for the future; the future has a face, and its name is "Sophia" the Robot. Developed by the visionaries at Hanson Robotics, she has transcended the role of a mere machine to become a worldwide phenomenon.

## How Was Sophia the Robot Created?
Sophia the Robot was designed to be the most human-like robot ever created. Her creator, \[Dr David Hanson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David\_Hanson\_(robotics_designer)), combined his experience as a Disney Imagineer with advanced material science to produce "Frubber".
This is a proprietary skin that moves and wrinkles just like yours. Inspired by the \[timeless elegance](https://www.hansonrobotics.com/sophia/) of Audrey Hepburn, Sophia was built to interact, educate, and entertain at the highest level.
Unlike standard industrial robots, Sophia is a social humanoid robot designed for eye contact and emotional mimicry. By using a computer vision algorithm, she can identify individuals and follow their movements.
This breaks the "fourth wall" of robotics and forces a connection. It is a calculated move to overcome the \[Uncanny Valley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uncanny_valley) and make humans feel a genuine link to an AI system.

### What is the "Uncanny Valley"?
The Uncanny Valley is a term used in social robotics to describe the dip in human comfort when a robot looks almost—but not exactly—like a person.
## Why Did Saudi Arabian Citizenship Cause A Scandal?
In a move that sent shockwaves through the global community, Sophia was declared an \[official Saudi citizen](https://observare.autonoma.pt/janus-net/en/janusnet/robot-citizenship-and-gender-inequality-the-case-of-sophia-the-robot-in-saudi-arabia/) in 2017. This was announced at the Future Investment Initiative Conference, making her the first robot citizen in history.
While some dismissed it as political choreography, the impact was undeniable. It forced people to think about the legal status of a machine versus a human being.
This event pushed the \[United Nations News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2017/10/568292) and the European Commission to fast-track discussions on sustainable development. It wasn't just a PR stunt; it was a wake-up call for the digital society.
We must now draft the rules for a world where humans and humanoid social robots coexist. Ignoring this shift is a mistake you cannot afford to make.
