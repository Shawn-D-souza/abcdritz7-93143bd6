## Why Did Saudi Arabian Citizenship Cause A Scandal?

In a move that sent shockwaves through the global community, Sophia was declared an \[official Saudi citizen](https://observare.autonoma.pt/janus-net/en/janusnet/robot-citizenship-and-gender-inequality-the-case-of-sophia-the-robot-in-saudi-arabia/) in 2017. This was announced at the Future Investment Initiative Conference, making her the first robot citizen in history.
While some dismissed it as political choreography, the impact was undeniable. It forced people to think about the legal status of a machine versus a human being.
This event pushed the \[United Nations News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2017/10/568292) and the European Commission to fast-track discussions on sustainable development. It wasn't just a PR stunt; it was a wake-up call for the digital society.
We must now draft the rules for a world where humans and humanoid social robots coexist. Ignoring this shift is a mistake you cannot afford to make.
