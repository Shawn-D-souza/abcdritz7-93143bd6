### The Step-by-Step Guide to Automating Gmail

  

1.  ### Setting Up Your Smart Filters (Starter)
    

Before Gemini AI can do its job, you have to give it a clear path. People call this first block the Starter.

![](/assets/uploads/t1yOI7A5dvoo7bev1HN3DnzeoVw.png)

1.  **How to navigate:** Go to your flow editor and select the first block: **"Step 1: When I get an email"**.
    
2.  **What to do:** Click the block to edit. Set conditions so the flow doesn't run on every spam message.
    
3.  **Filters to use:** Under conditions, filter by "Subject" or "Body". For instance, to track “password reset not working” issues, create a filter for that exact phrase. This will **automate** G**mail emails** related to support.
    

> _**The Why:** We do this to prevent the system from getting overwhelmed and to ensure only relevant **Customer data** reaches your Sheets._

  

1.  ### Using Gemini AI for Secret Weapon Labelling (AI-Powered Labels)
    

Now we bring in the brains. In the workflow, click **"Add step"** and select **"Add labels"**. This is a deep dive into the meaning of the message using Gemini AI.

![](/assets/uploads/udSNKVDnD28a9M0PGDJIxMsLmk.png)

1.  **How to navigate:** In the **"Add labels"** side panel, select the Email ID from Step 1. Enable the **"AI-powered labels"** toggle.
    
2.  **What to do:** Name your label (e.g., "Marketing-Test" or "Collab-test").
    
3.  **What prompt to use:** In the **"Description for Gemini"** box, use natural language to explain the logic.
    
4.  **Example prompt:** _"If the body of the email is referring to any collaboration opportunity with us for video creation or training programs, then mark it as collab-test."_
    

✅ **Best Practice:** Always toggle on the AI finding requirement so you apply labels only when Gemini AI finds a true match.

  

1.  ### How To Save Gmail Attachments To Google Drive Automatically
    

Searching through old emails for a single PDF is a complete waste of time. We use **Gemini AI** as our digital hunter to pull those files out and put them exactly where they belong.

*   **Step 1 — The Critical Filter:** **Here is where most people break the workflow:** You must set your **Starter** (Step 1) to "Specific Emails" and tick the box for **"Has attachments: Yes"**. If you leave it on "All emails," the system may try to run on messages that have nothing to save, leading to errors.
    
    ![](/assets/uploads/Mu7cN5s5oqTisu3ID5yZRVUGCA.jpg)
    
      
    
*   **Step 2 — Adding the Action:** In your workflow, add the step: **"Add email attachments to Drive"**.
    
    ![](/assets/uploads/Q3Mgf0eYK1kjFJZsEPLRtvuERo.png)
    
      
    
*   **Step 3 — Pick Your Destination:** Select your target folder (e.g., `Email Attachments` within your Google Workspace domain.
    
*   **Step 4 — Link the Variables:** In the "Gmail attachments" field, you must select the variable from Step 1. This tells the AI assistant exactly which files to grab.
    
    ![](/assets/uploads/ixx9vvL3VI8HDiVjlDOAJkpcRs.png)
    

  

> **The Why:** We use this specific filter to ensure the Super-Robot only wakes up when there is an actual file to process, keeping your **business workflows** clean and efficient.

✅ **Best Practice:** Create a dedicated folder for different types of Gmail attachments so they don’t just get dumped into a giant, messy pile in your Drive.

❌ **Bad Habit:** Manually downloading files to your desktop and then re-uploading them to Drive. It’s slow, it’s old-school, and it’s costing you money.

  

1.  ### How To Extract Gmail Data To Google Sheets Using Gemini AI
    

The final move in your power play is turning a messy conversation into structured data. We are going to automate Gmail emails so they populate a spreadsheet for you. This turns your inbox into a database that you can actually use to make decisions.

*   **Step 1 — Use the "Extract" Step:** In your workflow, add a new action called **"Extract"**.
    
    ![](/assets/uploads/dh6VQ80V8KOmWIeVlPiZL7J28.jpg)
    
      
    
*   **Step 2 — Define the Content:** Tell the **AI assistant** what to look at. Map the **"Body"** and **"Subject"** from your Gmail Starter (Step 1) into this block.
    
*   **Step 3 — Pick Your Variables:** You can choose predefined options like **"Sentiment Tone and Urgency"** or create custom fields. For example, tell **Gemini AI** to extract "More Info" (additional details to help your team) and the "Issue Reported."
    
*   **Step 4 — Log to Google Sheets:** Add the action **"Append Row"**. Select your target spreadsheet in your **Google Workspace domain**.
    
*   **Step 5 — Map the Columns:** Link your sheet columns (Name, Email, Issue, Tone, Urgency) to the variables extracted by **Gemini AI** in the previous step.

### 1\. How do I start to automate Gmail emails with Gemini AI?

Start by identifying one task, like saving invoices. Create a Gmail filter for those emails and then use Google Workspace Studio and Gemini AI to send those specific attachments to a Drive folder.

  

### 2\. Can Gemini AI access my Gmail?

Yes, Gemini AI can access your emails to provide AI assistance. However, Go**ogle’s security and privacy controls ensure your Customer data is not used to train public AI models. You maintain control via the privacy hub.**

  

### 3\. Is Gemini AI included with Google Workspace?

Yes, Gemini AI is now integrated into most Google Workspace plans, including Enterprise and [Education Plus](https://edu.google.com/workspace-for-education/editions/education-plus/). Institutions may also access these tools through a **Google AI Pro for Education license**.

  

### 4\. Can Gemini AI read my email attachments?

Yes, it can. This is a massive [multimodal breakthrough](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multimodal_learning). You can ask Gemini AI to provide **email summaries** of a PDF attachment and put that data into your Google Sheets.

  

### 5\. What happens if Gemini AI makes a mistake?

The Gemini AI is your co-pilot, not the captain. You should always set up a "Review" label. If the learning model output is unclear, the system moves the email to a folder for manual check.
