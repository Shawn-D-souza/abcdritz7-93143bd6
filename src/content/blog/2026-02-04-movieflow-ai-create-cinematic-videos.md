### Step 1: The "Auto" Duration Hack

Navigate to movieflow.ai and ignore the temptation to create small, choppy clips. Most amateurs waste hours generating 8-second snippets and trying to glue them together in a separate editor. That is a loser's game. Instead, set your duration to **"Auto".**

This setting is the secret sauce. It automatically calculates how much time is needed for the buildup, the climax, and the resolution. #### Example:

❌ **Bad/Weak Approach:** Setting a manual 10-second limit and hoping the AI can cram a whole story into it. (Result: A rushed, confusing mess).

✅ **Good/Strong Approach:** Selecting **"Auto"** and letting the AI's **parallel workflow** decide the pacing based on your 3-page script. (Result: A cinematic 120-second experience with a natural flow).

  

### Step 2: Use "Anchor" Prompting

The AI is a mirror of your own intelligence. If you give it a vague, lazy prompt, it will give you a vague, lazy video. To get professional results, you must use **"anchor" prompting.** This means identifying the specific "anchors"—fixed visual details—that the AI must keep consistent across every single shot.

By defining specific textures, colours, and objects, you give the **artificial intelligence** a visual compass. This prevents the "morphing" effect where characters change clothes or hair colour between scenes. You aren't just "asking" for a video; you are providing a detailed blueprint for the [asset generation pipeline](https://ltx.studio/blog/best-ai-character-generator) to follow.

  

#### Example: Anchoring Your Vision

❌ **Bad/Weak Prompting:** "The woman walks to the car." (The AI will guess the woman's age, the car's make, and the street's style, leading to total inconsistency).

✅ **Good/Strong Prompting:** "The woman with a neon-blue bob and a yellow raincoat walks toward a rusted 1967 Mustang in a rain-slicked neon alley." (This locks the hair, the coat, and the specific car model into the AI's memory).

  

### Step 3: SceneBuilder Intervention

If Scene 5 looks weird—maybe the character has an extra limb or the lighting shifts—don't scrap the whole project. That is what losers do. They give up at the first sign of a glitch. Successful creators use the **SceneBuilder tool** to perform surgical edits on the fly.

This tool allows you to isolate a specific segment of your 2-minute video and "re-roll" just that shot without losing the other 29 perfect scenes. You can adjust the prompt for that specific block or swap the entire visual while keeping the audio and timing synced. Remember: perfection is a myth that keeps you from publishing. In the business of content, "good enough to ship" beats "perfect and unreleased" every single time.

## How To Fix Glitches Like A Pro

Yes, sometimes the AI gives a character six fingers. Stop complaining and fix it.

*   **Lower Motion Intensity:** If your video looks like a fever dream, you’ve set the motion too high. Set it to **4 or 5**.
    
*   **Repetition Is Key:** If the character’s hat disappears, mention the hat in every single scene block of your script.
    
*   **Natural Voice:** Use **voice cloning** to turn text into natural voice-overs so your dialogue doesn't sound like a robot from 2010.
