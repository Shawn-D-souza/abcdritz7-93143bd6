Automation makes running a business easier by automating tasks and saving time. For example, think of the **"sorry I missed you"** email from a salesperson, a birthday coupon from a restaurant, or a reminder about items in your shopping cart from an e-commerce website. 

Business Process Automation is crucial for success. According to Gleanster research, [almost 80% of top-performing companies](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/marketecs_did-you-know-almost-80-of-top-performing-activity-7174018970969112576-RmPO) have used automation for over two years. 

But automation isn’t just for big corporations anymore. Small businesses can and should use automation in every part of their operations, from attracting leads to finalizing sales, assisting customers, and handling routine office tasks. By automating repetitive processes, small business owners can focus on important goals like growing their business. 

In this guide, you’ll discover 10 things every small business should automate and how simple it can be to automate business processes.

In the face of limited resources, small businesses must balance creativity with consistency in their customer support approach. After all, the success of any business depends on its **potential customers** and their willingness to make repeat purchases.

Automating customer support lets you treat customers as if you had more time. This might seem odd, but customers get frustrated by hard-to-find contact links and slow responses. Replying quickly keeps customers happy, retains them, and increases their value over time. Just ensure responses are accurate and on-brand.

1.  ## Supply Chain Management
    

Businesses should definitely automate their [supply chain management](https://ritz7.com/blog/inventory-management-systems-gain-ambani-level-efficiency). 1.  ## Net Promoter Score (NPS) feedback
    

Every business needs to hear from its customers. But getting that feedback can take a lot of time, especially if you're doing it all by hand. There's a risk of asking for feedback at the wrong time or not asking at all. That's where NPS (Net Promoter Score) feedback comes in. It's a valuable tool for understanding how satisfied your customers are and how likely they are to recommend your business to others. 

By collecting NPS feedback, you can identify areas for improvement, address any issues, and ultimately strengthen your customer relationships. Plus, it helps you shape your future marketing strategies based on what your customers really want

1.  ## Appointment Scheduling
    

Small business automation also includes an appointment scheduler. Owners should use calendar integration tools like:

*   Calendly
    
*   Picktime
    

These tools are often free or very cheap. They help you manage appointments without having to talk to each client. You can also learn useful things by adding questions to the scheduler.

This helps you understand what clients need and prepare better for meetings. You can also get their email addresses and phone numbers and learn how they heard about your business.

Additionally, these tools can streamline communication by enabling follow-up emails, email reminders, and even automated follow-up emails after appointments. You can manage your email list effectively and even send a confirmation email to clients after scheduling, ensuring a seamless and professional experience.

1.  ## Review Management 
    

Keeping up with customer reviews and customer feedback can be challenging, especially if you're listed on many platforms with limited control. Unhappy customers often share their experiences publicly, allowing you to identify issues, find their causes, and solve them.

Positive reviews are a great chance to connect with happy customers, engage prospective clients, and boost team morale. Sharing these reviews on social media can also improve engagement rates and build stronger community connections.

However, keeping track of reviews manually isn’t practical. Affordable [business automation tools](https://ritz7.com/blog/custom-software-tools-a-tailored-solution-for-sme-success) make review monitoring and management easier, ensuring no feedback is missed.

These tools are a powerful tool for customer service teams and customer service representatives, helping them respond quickly and effectively. Alternatively, hiring a top-notch automation service provider can streamline the process further.

1.  ## Daily Reporting
    

Automating daily reporting is crucial for businesses that depend on digital channels for revenue and sales. This involves gathering data from various online sources such as:

*   Google Analytics
    
*   Google Search Console
    
*   Google Ads
    
*   Facebook Ads
    

By utilizing Google Data Studio, you can merge this data and set up automated email reports, either daily or weekly. This automation not only reduces the workload of sales teams but also minimizes human error, ensuring accurate and reliable insights. Having a comprehensive overview of your performance and data is essential for making informed decisions.

  

1.  ## Customer relationship management (CRM) system.
    

Every small business should automate its CRM system. This not only helps track all customer interactions but also enhances customer service by streamlining the sales process and enabling better management of sales funnels.

An automated CRM can also simplify lead tracking and generate detailed reports for sales reps, helping them focus on potential sales rather than manual data entry.

In addition to tracking programs, invoicing, and customer satisfaction scores, automated CRM systems provide insights that directly impact profits.

Before choosing a CRM, research different [software options](https://ritz7.com/blog/custom-web-solutions-the-growth-engine-for-smes) to find the best fit for your business needs. 1.  ## Email Marketing Automation
    

If I had to pick one essential automation for small businesses, it would be email marketing. While it’s a popular choice, its potential for streamlining business operations is often overlooked.

By automating email marketing, businesses can reduce manual tasks and save precious time, allowing them to focus on nurturing both existing and potential clients.

With high conversion rates, email marketing is a powerful way to build a community around your product and brand. Automated campaigns can handle the otherwise time-consuming process of engaging with your contact list. Some examples include:

*   Welcome emails
    
*   Abandoned cart emails
    
*   Birthday/anniversary emails
    

Using an email marketing platform powered by machine learning, businesses can create personalized email marketing campaigns with ease. Features like automated reminders, automatic email responses, and appointment confirmation emails ensure seamless communication, enhancing efficiency and customer satisfaction.
